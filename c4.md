# Proposed Implementation Plan for Issues #5, #6, and #7

## Overview
This document summarizes the proposed updates to the Rock Talk 1.0 academic paper to incorporate scientific positioning, an operational origin story, and a formal validation framework, as suggested in the recent open issues.

## Detailed Plan (Prose)

### 1. Reframing and Scientific Positioning (Issue #5)
The primary update involves shifting the tone of the paper from definitive to exploratory. We will replace assertive claims with hypothesis-based language. For instance, rather than stating that Rock Talk "improves alignment," we will state that we hypothesize it may improve alignment in specific contexts.

A new "Proposed Validation Framework" section will be added. This section will introduce two key metrics:
- **Token-to-Intent Ratio (TIR):** Total Tokens divided by Intended Semantic Objectives.
- **Semantic Density Index (SDI):** Unique Semantic Concepts divided by Total Tokens.

We will also outline three controlled experiments targeting prompt efficiency, multi-agent coordination, and human comprehension load.

### 2. Operational Origin and Primary Source Citation (Issue #6)
We will add a "Motivating Incident" section in the Introduction. This will formally document the production server error (HTTP 500) that served as the catalyst for Rock Talk. Crucially, we will include the operator's original narrative ("Me software developer...") as a formal primary-source citation. This preserves the project's authentic history while framing it within the academic context of "experience reports" and "operator logs."

### 3. Bidirectional Protocol Evaluation (Issue #7)
The evaluation section will be expanded to compare Rock Talk against one-sided compression methods (such as the Caveman skill). We will propose a three-arm study comparing:
1. Baseline (Conversational)
2. One-sided compression (Model output only)
3. Bidirectional Rock Talk (Trained user + compressed output)

We hypothesize that Condition 3 will demonstrate the highest efficiency and accuracy, particularly in complex agentic workflows.
