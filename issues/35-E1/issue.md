## The Connection
McLuhan (1964) argued that the channel of communication shapes meaning independent of content. The same message delivered via different media produces different effects — not because the words changed, but because the medium changed.

This is the same claim Rock Talk makes about transformer attention:

> The same semantic intent delivered in Rock Talk hits the attention mechanism differently than in natural language. Not because the meaning changed, but because the medium changed.

This is not metaphor. It is the same principle applied to a new substrate.
