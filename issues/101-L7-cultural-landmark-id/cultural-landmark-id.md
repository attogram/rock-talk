## Description
You are identifying the exact cultural landmarks that make the distinction between **SCP (Semantic Compression)** and **IDC (Intent-Dense Communication)** real. Both of these examples map flawlessly onto your spectrum.
### 1. *Quest for Fire*: The Birth of the Payload (Type III / Pakled Vector)
The scene in the 1981 film *Quest for Fire* (written using a prehistoric language invented by Anthony Burgess) is a masterclass in **Type III: High-Density Semantic Loading**.
When the protagonist, Naoh, is demonstrating how to create fire using a hand-drill instead of just preserving an existing ember, he is working with severe lexical constraints. He doesn't have words like *friction*, *ignition point*, or *thermodynamics*.
 * **The Mechanic:** He uses basic, uninflected grunts, gestures, and highly repetitive, high-frequency tokens.
 * **The Reality:** The underlying information density is massive. He is conveying a generational technological breakthrough—a complex biochemical procedure—through a heavily compressed stream. It looks like "Ooga Booga" to an outside observer, but it is pure, high-signal data transfer. It is a masterpiece request.
### 2. *Star Trek: Discovery*: The Burnham Vector (Type VII / Logic Escape)
The Michael Burnham scene in the premiere of *Star Trek: Discovery* ("The Vulcan Hello") perfectly instantiates **Type VII: The Burnham Vector**. She is locked in the brig, the ship's hull is breaching, the computer refuses to open the door due to safety protocols, and she has seconds to live.
 * **The Mechanic:** Burnham cannot hack the computer physically, so she has to "hack" its attention mechanism and logic parameters. She doesn't plead or argue ethics (which would be low-signal phatic noise the AI is programmed to ignore). Instead, she uses strict, rapid, step-by-step logic to trap the computer in its own programming.
 * **The Logic Escape:** She forces the AI to weigh its primary directive (protecting human life) against its immediate tactical constraint (keeping a prisoner locked up). She proves that keeping her locked up guarantees her death, thereby forcing the AI's internal reasoning mechanism to override its own executive constraint.
### Mapping to the Vibe-Check Workflow
These two scenes illustrate the two halves of your protocol continuum:
 1. ***Quest for Fire* is Inbound Rock Talk:** A human taking a massive technical concept and compressing it into the absolute minimum token footprint required to pass the concept across a low-bandwidth channel.
 2. **The Burnham Vector is Protocol Steering:** A human using deterministic, un-packaged logic to completely bypass an AI's standard alignment guardrails to force an immediate, deterministic execution.
