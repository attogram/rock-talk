## Description
Fun read—half satire, half genuine prompt-engineering idea. Real signal: stripping conversational filler from prompts can save tokens and sometimes sharpen focus, and the "Caveman" framing makes that intuitive.

Weak spots: most citations (Cytherians, Pakleds, Kevin Malone, "Frising 2025," etc.) are decorative pop-culture/joke references dressed as scholarship, not actual support for the claims. The "SDI/TIR" metrics and "20-50% savings" are asserted, not measured—no experiments, no data. Also worth noting: terse prompts can *increase* ambiguity, forcing the model to guess intent, which may hurt accuracy on complex tasks—the opposite of "better alignment."

Bottom line: cute concept paper, but it's an essay built around a meme, not a validated protocol. Treat "Rock Talk" as a fun prompting tip (be concise, lead with the ask) rather than research with real backing.
