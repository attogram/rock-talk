## Description
Got it — Rock Culture v0.2. A few quick notes:

**Strengths**
- The taxonomy table is clean and immediately useful as a reference. The SCP/IDC formalization gives the archetypes scientific legs beyond just being pop culture riffs.
- Types VIII, IX, X (Watney, Heptapod B, Nathan) are genuinely interesting additions — especially Heptapod B as a theoretical ceiling for intent-loading.
- The cultural bias section (Section 4) is appropriately humble.

**Things to tighten**
- The table in the ROCK TALK section has 10 types but the prose introduction says "7 categories" — that's a direct inconsistency that needs fixing, and the Rock Talk paper (Section 4) also says "7 formal categories."
- Type VI (Keyrock/Proficiency Cloaking) is interesting but it's doing double duty — it's both a communication style *and* an adversarial attack vector. That tension might deserve its own section rather than being squeezed into the taxonomy.
- The Nathan Axiom (Type X) feels underdeveloped compared to the others. One sentence of analysis vs. several for the earlier types.

**Biggest gap**
This paper is referenced heavily by Rock Talk for "worked examples and archetype efficiency benchmarks" (Section 2.1), but those benchmarks don't actually appear here yet. That's a forward reference that currently goes nowhere.

Is that planned for a future version?
